Focus Shield — Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

Summary

Focus Shield does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data. Everything happens locally in your browser. There are no servers, no analytics, no accounts, and no tracking of any kind.

Data Collection

Focus Shield collects zero user data. Specifically:

We do not collect browsing history. We do not collect search queries. We do not collect personal information. We do not collect usage analytics. We do not use cookies. We do not transmit any data to any server.

Local Storage

The extension stores two pieces of data locally on your device using Chrome's built-in chrome.storage.local API:

Block count: A simple integer tracking how many times content was blocked (e.g., "47 times protected"). Block log: Timestamps (not URLs or content) of the last 100 blocks, used to calculate your day streak. This data never leaves your device and is automatically deleted if you uninstall the extension.

Network Requests

Focus Shield makes zero network requests of its own. It does not communicate with any external server, API, or analytics service. All keyword matching, content scanning, and blocking logic runs entirely within your browser.

Permissions

The extension requires broad permissions (host_permissions for all URLs) solely to scan page content for NSFW material. These permissions are used exclusively for local content analysis and blocking. No data from any page is ever transmitted externally.

Third Parties

Focus Shield does not integrate with any third-party services, advertising networks, or analytics platforms.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted here with a new date. The extension will never collect personal data regardless of policy updates.

Contact

For questions about this privacy policy, open an issue on the GitHub repository or contact the developer via the Chrome Web Store listing.
